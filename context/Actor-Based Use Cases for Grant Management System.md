## 1. CEO Use Case: Review, Approve, and Guide Investment Decisions

### Primary Actor

CEO — Chief Executive Officer

### Goal

Ensure that grantmaking and investment decisions are strategically aligned, well-reviewed, and approved at the right decision points.

### Trigger

A strategy proposal, concept note, application review, or investment memo is ready for CEO review.

### Main Flow

1. CEO receives a proposal, concept note, or investment memo from KMD or AL. 
2. CEO reviews the document for strategic alignment, investment rationale, risks, and organizational fit.
3. CEO refines or comments on the document.
4. CEO approves the document or requests revisions.
5. For concept or memo review meetings, CEO selects up to two participants.
6. CEO and KMD present strategic materials to ALs for validation when relevant.
7. CEO provides written questions or comments on investment memos.
8. CEO records approval, rejection, or conditional approval in the system.

### Expected Output

A documented CEO decision that moves the initiative forward, sends it back for revision, or rejects it.

### Key System Features

- Review and approval dashboard
- Commenting and revision workflow
- Decision log
- Participant selection for review meetings
- Notification system
- Access to strategy, concept notes, and investment memos

## 2. AL Use Case: Manage Initiative Pipeline from Sourcing to Onboarding

### Primary Actor

AL — Area Lead

### Goal

Identify, assess, advance, and onboard initiatives or organizations aligned with the thematic strategy.

### Trigger

An approved thematic strategy is ready to be implemented within an area.

### Main Flow

1. AL identifies potential initiatives, organizations, or individuals with support from AT.
2. AL enters opportunities into the CRM-style pipeline.
3. AL coordinates scoping calls for promising opportunities.
4. AL assesses strategic fit using Investment Criteria Set 1.
5. AL requests concept notes and institutional decks from high-fit organizations.
6. AL prepares concept notes for initiatives with strong solution strength and learning value.
7. AL sends concept notes to CEO.
8. AL schedules concept review meetings.
9. If approved, AL requests the full application form.
10. AL informs KMD when the application is received.
11. AL works with KMD to produce the application review report.
12. AL writes the investment memo and nominates peer reviewers.
13. AL responds to reviewer and CEO comments.
14. AL schedules memo review meetings.
15. Once approved, AL informs AD that legal due diligence should begin.
16. AL sends legal due diligence requirements to the organization.
17. AL schedules the kick-off meeting after approval.
18. AL, AT, and ORG review KPIs, timing, scope, reporting, and support options.
### Expected Output

A complete and traceable initiative record from sourcing through approval, legal review, and onboarding.

### Key System Features

- CRM pipeline
- Contact management
- Scoping call notes
- Criteria-based assessment
- Concept note workflow
- Application request tracking
- Memo drafting and review
- Peer review management
- Meeting scheduling
- Onboarding checklist

## 3. AD Use Case: Manage Legal Due Diligence

### Primary Actor

AD — Administrative Director

### Goal

Coordinate and validate the legal due diligence process before an investment or grant is finalized.

### Trigger

AL informs AD that an approved initiative is ready to begin legal due diligence.

### Main Flow

1. AD receives notification from AL that legal due diligence should begin.
2. AD identifies the required legal documents, forms, and support materials.
3. AD sends the requirements to AL.
4. AL shares the requirements with the external organization.
5. ORG submits completed forms and documents.
6. AD reviews the submitted materials.
7. AD validates the documentation with the trust.
8. If revisions are needed, AD marks the submission as incomplete and requests corrections.
9. Once validated, AD marks legal due diligence as complete.

### Expected Output

A completed and validated legal due diligence package.

### Key System Features

- Legal due diligence checklist
- Document request management
- Upload portal
- Status tracking
- Revision requests
- Trust validation log
- Completion approval

## 4. TL Use Case: Provide Technical Review and Support

### Primary Actor

TL — Tech Lead

### Goal

Assess technical feasibility, risks, and support needs for initiatives that involve technology, digital products, data systems, or technical implementation.

### Trigger

An initiative under review requires technical assessment or technical support planning.

### Main Flow

1. TL is invited by CEO, AL, or AT to review a concept note, application, or investment memo.
2. TL reviews the initiative’s technical components, implementation plan, risks, and capacity.
3. TL provides comments or recommendations.
4. TL identifies technical risks, dependencies, infrastructure needs, or product constraints.
5. TL recommends whether technical support is needed during onboarding or implementation.
6. TL contributes to the support plan reviewed during the kick-off meeting.
7. TL records technical observations and support recommendations in the system.

### Expected Output

A documented technical assessment and support recommendation.

### Key System Features

- Technical review assignment
- Access to concept notes, applications, and memos
- Commenting workflow
- Technical risk checklist
- Support recommendation field
- Integration with onboarding support plan

## 5. KMD Use Case: Develop Strategy and Process Knowledge for Investment Decisions

### Primary Actor

KMD — Knowledge Management Director

### Goal

Create the strategic, methodological, and learning foundations that guide investment decisions and support evidence-based review.

### Trigger

The organization needs to define or update strategy, investment criteria, thematic strategy, ToC, investment thesis, learning agenda, or application review reports.

### Main Flow

1. KMD develops proposals for the company strategy, investment process map, or investment criteria.
2. KMD develops the methodological proposal for thematic strategy, ToC, investment thesis, and learning agenda.
3. CEO reviews, refines, and approves the proposal.
4. CEO and KMD present the proposal to ALs for validation.
5. KMD supports the processing of application information under internal protocols.
6. KMD collaborates with AL to produce the application review report using the approved format.
7. KMD ensures information is structured for learning, comparison, and future decision-making.
8. KMD stores finalized documents and knowledge outputs in the system.

### Expected Output

Approved strategic frameworks, validated investment criteria, and structured knowledge products that support investment decisions.

### Key System Features

- Strategy document workspace
- Version control
- Methodology templates
- Learning agenda tracking
- Application review report templates
- Knowledge repository
- Data processing protocols
- Cross-area validation workflow

## 6. AT Use Case: Support Area Lead in Sourcing, Screening, and Onboarding

### Primary Actor

AT — Area Team

### Goal

Support the AL in identifying opportunities, managing information, conducting scoping, and onboarding approved organizations.

### Trigger

AL begins sourcing or managing initiatives within an area.

### Main Flow

1. AT helps AL identify initiatives, organizations, or individuals aligned with thematic strategy.
2. AT gathers public information and network intelligence.
3. AT enters opportunity details into the CRM-type list.
4. AT helps prepare summaries of up to 100 words.
5. AT supports scoping calls and records notes.
6. AT helps organize documents, concept notes, decks, and application materials.
7. AT supports the AL during onboarding.
8. AT participates in the kick-off meeting with the organization.
9. AT helps review KPIs, timing, scope, reporting conditions, and support options.

### Expected Output

A well-documented pipeline of opportunities and a smooth onboarding process for approved organizations.

### Key System Features

- CRM data entry
- Opportunity profiles
- Contact records
- Scoping call notes
- Task assignments
- Document upload and organization
- Meeting minutes
- Onboarding checklist

## 7. ORG Use Case: Submit Information and Participate in Grant Review

### Primary Actor

ORG — External Organization or Initiative  
In venture building cases, this role may be replaced by the AL.

### Goal

Submit the required information, participate in review processes, complete legal due diligence, and align on implementation expectations.

### Trigger

AL identifies the organization or initiative as a potential fit and requests information.

### Main Flow

1. ORG participates in a short scoping call with AL and AT.
2. If invited, ORG submits a concept note using the required format.
3. ORG shares an institutional pitch or deck when available.
4. If approved for due diligence, ORG completes the full application form.
5. ORG submits supporting materials requested by AL and KMD.
6. During legal due diligence, ORG completes required forms and uploads legal documents.
7. If revisions are requested, ORG corrects and resubmits documents.
8. Once approved, ORG joins the kick-off meeting.
9. ORG reviews and agrees on KPIs, timing, scope, reporting conditions, and support options.

### Expected Output

A complete submission package, validated legal documentation, and agreed onboarding conditions.

### Key System Features

- External submission portal
- Concept note upload
- Application form
- Document checklist
- Legal due diligence upload
- Revision request notifications
- Meeting participation records
- KPI and reporting agreement record
