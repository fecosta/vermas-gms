Actors
	•	CEO (Chief Executive Officer)
	•	KMD (Knowledge Management Director)
	•	AL (Area Lead – VP/Director level)
	•	AT (Area Team)
	•	AD (Administrative Director)
	•	TL (Tech Lead) (appears as support option during onboarding)
	•	ORG (External organization / initiative applying for or receiving funding)

---

CEO — Use Cases
	1.	Approve strategic documents
	•	Review/refine and approve: investment process map, investment criteria, ToC/investment thesis/learning agenda.
	2.	Validate strategy with Area Leads
	•	Co-present strategy items to ALs; record validation outcomes.
	3.	Review concept notes
	•	Receive concept notes; provide feedback; decide whether to advance.
	4.	Select participants for review meetings
	•	Choose up to 2 participants for concept review / memo review meetings.
	5.	Respond with written questions/comments on memos
	•	Send structured questions to AL; track responses and closure.
	6.	Governance oversight dashboard
	•	See pipeline status by stage (sourcing → screening → due diligence → legal → onboarding), decision history, bottlenecks, upcoming meetings.

---

KMD — Use Cases
	1.	Draft strategic frameworks
	•	Create: investment process map, investment criteria guidelines, methodological proposal (ToC/thesis/learning agenda).
	2.	Submit for CEO review and track versions
	•	Maintain versioning, approvals, and “current active” strategy documents.
	3.	Process application information under protocols
	•	Receive “application received” notification from AL; store/process documents with required controls.
	4.	Co-author application review report
	•	Collaborate with AL on ver+ format report, with structured sections and sign-offs.
	5.	Knowledge capture & learning agenda tracking
	•	Link each initiative to learning goals; track evidence gathered during due diligence and post-onboarding reporting.

---

AL — Use Cases
	1.	Manage sourcing pipeline
	•	Create and maintain CRM-style records (initiative/org/person, contacts, summary ≤100 words).
	2.	Coordinate scoping calls
	•	Schedule/run scoping calls; log notes; qualify fit against criteria sets.
	3.	Request and collect screening materials
	•	Request concept note (up to 2 pages) + institutional deck; track submissions and completeness.
	4.	Create concept note for concept review
	•	Draft “why yes/why not” concept note aligned to Investment Criteria Set 2; submit to CEO.
	5.	Schedule concept review meeting
	•	Build agenda (≤30 min per initiative); invite CEO-selected participants; capture minutes.
	6.	Initiate full application
	•	After CEO approval, request full application form; track completion and reminders.
	7.	Produce application review report
	•	Notify KMD upon receipt; co-produce report; manage review cycles.
	8.	Write peer-reviewed memo
	•	Draft memo aligned to Investment Criteria Set 3; nominate two peer reviewers; incorporate feedback; finalize.
	9.	Run memo review / investment committee workflow
	•	Send memo to CEO; respond to CEO questions; schedule memo review meeting; capture minutes.
	10.	Trigger legal due diligence

	•	Notify AD that legal DD starts; send doc list/forms/support materials to ORG; track status.

	11.	Run onboarding kick-off

	•	Schedule kick-off; review KPIs/timing/scope; confirm reporting conditions and available support options (MEL/Tech/strategic).

---

AT — Use Cases
	1.	Support sourcing & data entry
	•	Help identify initiatives; enrich CRM records; maintain contact info hygiene.
	2.	Support scoping calls
	•	Participate in calls; capture structured notes; tag learnings to thematic strategy.
	3.	Support onboarding implementation
	•	Participate in kick-off; help define KPIs and reporting cadence; coordinate support requests (MEL/Tech/strategic).
	4.	Operational tracking
	•	Maintain timelines, tasks, and dependencies (e.g., upcoming submissions, missing documents, meeting actions).

---

AD — Use Cases
	1.	Start legal due diligence workflow
	•	Receive notification from AL once memo is approved; open a legal DD case.
	2.	Define required legal documents & forms
	•	Publish a checklist per ORG; include templates and guidance.
	3.	Validate submissions
	•	Review ORG-submitted documents; request revisions; keep audit trail of changes.
	4.	Validate with the trust
	•	Route/record trust validation; mark legal DD approved/rejected; if rejected, restart the flow with tracked reasons.
	5.	Compliance reporting
	•	View legal DD status across all grants; produce exportable compliance summaries.

---

TL — Use Cases (support role referenced in onboarding)
	1.	Provide technical support intake
	•	Receive onboarding “Tech support” requests; clarify scope; accept/decline; schedule.
	2.	Track delivery of technical support
	•	Tasks, milestones, artifacts produced; link to grant/initiative record.
	3.	Coordinate with AL/AT and ORG
	•	Shared workspace for requirements, status updates, and sign-off.

---

ORG (External Organization) — Use Cases
	1.	Maintain organization profile
	•	Org info, contacts, decks, legal identity details; update point-of-contact.
	2.	Submit screening materials
	•	Upload concept note and deck when requested; track status and feedback.
	3.	Submit full application form
	•	Complete ver+ application form; upload required attachments; respond to clarifications.
	4.	Complete legal due diligence package
	•	Receive AD’s checklist; upload documents; fill forms; iterate revisions until validated.
	5.	Participate in onboarding kick-off
	•	Review/agree to KPIs, scope, reporting conditions, and support options.
	6.	Ongoing reporting (post-onboarding)
	•	Submit periodic reports aligned with agreed KPIs and cadence; attach evidence where needed.

---

	2.	Comment and ask questions

	4.	Provide final review sign-off
